Previous Tournaments

  • Sep 12

    Dougherty Valley High School VEX Robotics Tournament

    The Dougherty Valley High School Tournament was our first tournament, and it was a great learning experience. We finished in the Round of 16 at this tournament, and there were several problems we had to fix. Firstly, our robot was constantly disconnecting during critical moments. Secondly, our intake wasn't efficient, and worked very on and off. Lastly, our tray was making a machine gun type noise, when deploying cubes in the scoring zone, due to the metal meshing against the gears.

  • Oct 6

    Second Tournament in Milpitas

    This tournament was a bit of a faliure, as not only did we rank as one of the bottom most teams after qualifiers, but we didn't qualify for the playoffs at all. This was primarily because our last minute changes had prohibited our intake from moving, and our deploying mechanism was stuck. All we had for most of this competition was a drivetrain, and we suspected that the heavyweight of 9 cubes was too much for one motor operating on our tray mechanism.

  • Oct 26

    First League Tournament in Stockton

    This was by far our best performance thus far in the year, as we went 4-1, scoring a maximum of 48 points. We were very proud of our performance, but we knew that our driver practice was lackluster. Our intake was working efficiently, and our tray was moving up and down without any problems, so we were glad that problem was solved.

  • Nov 16

    Our Second League Tournament in Stockton

    Our primary driver couldn't make it to this competition, and we had last minute crams for backup driver driving practice, which we should've practiced beforehand. We went 3-2 in this competition, and our intake was not at full strength. During the full course of our 5 matches, our tray could only intake a maximum of 7 cubes, and it struggled to deploy all the way. After our final match, we found out that there was a small piece at the bottom of the tray, obstructing more cubes from entering.

  • Jan 10

    Last Tournament in Modesto

    Although we won excellence in this tournament, our notebook could've used some work. In addition, we realized our strongest driver strategy was not to alliance with a strong tray bot, but rather with a stronger tower bot, so that our scoring is multiplied, and much more efficient. Other than that, all parts of our robot worked very well, and we look forward to adding a tower mechanism in addition to our consistent tray bot.
